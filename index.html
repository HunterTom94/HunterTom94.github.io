My name is Yinan (Tom) Xuan. I am currently a third year PhD student in the Division Of Biological Sciences at University of California, San Diego (UCSD). I received my undergraduate training at UCSD in major of Physiology and Neuroscience with a minor of Cognitive Science. During my Ph.D. training, I worked with Professor Jing Wang to study the neurobiology of olfaction in Drosophila Melanogaster, also known as the fruit flies.

In the Wang Lab, I have developed and integrated an image processing software to extract individual cell activity information from videos of flattened fruit fly guts. I have also devised and implemented an olfactory virtual reality system to measure odor guided behaviors in fruit flies. I also had the opportunity to participate in building a custom three-photon fluorescent imaging rig. In this process, I learned free-space optics while aligning ultrafast laser for multiphoton (two & three photon) microscopy. I also helped build a custom (2nd order) auto-correlator to characterize the pulse-width of ultrafast emission from an Optical Parametric Amplifier.

During the process of designing and implementing tools and algorithms to record and assess biological phenomena, I realized that my true passion really lies in building novel devices that can be used by humans to record activities and physiological states, thereby impacting their health and everyday lives positively. Therefore, I plan to graduate with a M.S. degree in June, 2020 and I am currently applying to other Ph.D. programs that allows me to do research in the field of ubiquitous computing. My research interests lie at the intersection of ubiquitous computing, personal tracking and health informatics. I am interested in designing and implementing novel systems and related algorithms to collect users’ activity and physiological information in a naturalistic way, and leverage this information as inputs to computing systems.
