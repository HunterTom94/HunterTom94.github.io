Feedback Temperature Control System for Fruit Fly Surgery

controlling components

Top to bottom: thermocouple sensor, Peltier plate, (thermal paste), heat sink, fan

A feedback control system is built to keep the temperature of a metal holding rig cold enough to anesthetize the fly for surgeries such as opening the head or the back under the microscope.

Keeping flies at a temperature around ice point is a well known practice to anesthetize flies with minimum side effects. A Peltier plate is used to cool the metal rig that holds the fly. According to Peltier effect, When current passes through the Peltier plate, heat will be transferred from one side of the Peltier plate to the other side. However, if the heat from the hot side is not dissipated promptly, the heat will be transferred back to the cold side simply due to second law of thermodynamics. A heat sink with a fan is thus used to help dissipate the heat from the hot side.

A thermocouple is used to monitor the temperature of the metal rig. Its readout is then amplified and read via an Arduino Nano's analog input. At the same time, a potentiometer is used as a voltage divider to provide variable voltage inputs to the Arduino Nano analog input, which can be converted to a target temperature with code. The Arduino Nano compares the temperature of the metal rig with the target temperature and cuts Peltier plate's current supply via a N-type MOSFET if the rig's temperature is below the target temperature. Finally, a display is added to show the current temperature of rig and the current target temperature. A PCB is designed to integrate all the components.

This project is in light of a similar project. The original project used a commercially available temperature controller, while I built the temperature controller with a much less monetary cost.
