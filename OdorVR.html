Olfactory Virtual Reality for Flies

Overview of the setup

Zoom in of tethered fly (Fly will be lowered during experiment)

This project is an olfactory VR system to measure odor-guided behaviors in Drosophila melanogaster (fruit fly). The system consists of three parts: a spherical treadmill, an olfactometer (to deliver odor in a controlled manner), and a feedback system (to update the delivery of odor based on the flyâ€™s position in the virtual world).

This system is designed to be compatible with multi-photon imaging so that the neuron activity in fly's brain can be recorded together with its behavioral activities.

Spherical Treadmill

The ball treadmill tracking system is initially invented by Buchner in 1976 and is now a relatively mature technique, where an animal is tethered and positioned on top of a floating sphere pushed up by gentle air stream from the bottom.

As the animal walks, the sphere is turned and the animal is interpreted to be walking on a fictive two-dimensional plane. The walking trajectory of the animal is inferred from the rotation of the sphere.

Video recording is used to determine the rotation of the sphere and the movement of the tethered fly. Since 1976, methods have evolved over time , in this project I used FicTrac , an open-source software that infers animals walking trajectory from video information of only one camera.

Olfactometer

Odors are diluted before delivered to the animal. Two mass flow controllers (MFC) are used to control odor concentration. One controller sets the flow rate of the air stream carrying the odor, while the other sets the flow rate of the air stream that serves to dilute the odor. PID readout (Pulse Mode)

Code is written in Python to communicate with MFC through serial ports and is integrated with closed-loop control, which will be discussed in the following section. The code takes the final total flow rate and the target dilution factor as input, and find the best match of the combination of dilution MFC flow rate and odor MFC flow rate. Such flow rates are subsequently sent as commands to both MFCs as updated setpoints.

Alicat MC Series MFCs are used because of their accuracy, fast response time and ability to be controlled via serial communication. After tuning PD parameters, the two controllers are able to reach a response time less than 100 milliseconds when the input is compressed air at 30 psi.

The odor air stream and the dilution air stream are mixed at an odor mixing chamber that supports up to 8 odor inputs. One metal grid is positioned at the output of the mixing chamber to increase the turbulence for better mixing. The diameter of the output port is 13.7mm. When the final output flow rate is set to be 500 Standard Cubic CentiMeters(SCCM), the speed of air stream coming out of the port would be 5.6 cm/s.

Photoionization detector (PID) has been used to determine the performance of the olfactometer. The odor can be delivered well in pulse, but the concentration may fluctuate a lot if the odor is delivered continuously. This is potentially due to the depletion of odor inside the odor bottle.
